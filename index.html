    Welcome to Labro

    In the heart of Italy is a small village called “Labro”, located on the border between Lazio and Umbria, on a wooded hill looking out over the lake of Piediluco and Riete. Labro is located about 80 miles from Rome which takes about two hours due to the heavy traffic and congestion.

    The place offers a castle, three pizzerias, three restaurants, a mini market, a bar and an antique shops, which open only for the summer.

    The current castle as it stands today was used until the fifteenth century for defense as an ancient fortress. The castle is still being inhabited by Vitelleschi Nobili family, which is still fully furnished with furniture, objects and paintings handed down from generation to generation. Of particular interest is the family archive, intact and provided particularly for documents that go back about one thousands year and tell the story of Labro and resident family to this day. It is possible to take a guided tour of the picturesque Castle.

    In addition to the beauty of the village and the surrounding nature, the town is also a good stopping place to reach other places of interest such as: Leonessa, Terminillo, Carsulae and the Marmore waterfall and much more.
